Character Series

Humility

What is humility?

Easier to see than to define

It is not an action word but is clearly revealed in our actions

“humility is not thinking less of yourself, it's thinking of yourself less.”

- C.S. Lewis

However, we're naturally prone to place ourselves ahead of others because normally we care more about ourselves than we do others

Which means over time we develop an unhealthy sense of our own self-worth

Humility is a state of mind. It's an awareness of where we really fit in the big picture of life. It's a realization that "I am" not the center of life… the world does not revolve around "me"

Humility is coming to terms with who we are in the light of who God is, and then “living out” that reality in our day to-day lives

Reflection

In which of the following areas have you found difficult to display humility?

  • Sports/games
  • With family
  • When someone butts in line ahead of you
  • In hobbies
  • Heavy traffic
  • When being given an honor/award
  • Interactions with entitled people

Scripture

Luke 18:9-14

1. What kind of person is Jesus speaking about in the Luke 18:9 parable?

2. Who was more honest with God about where he fit in the big picture of life, the Pharisee or the tax collector?

Why was that individual able to see himself more accurately and clearly in God's estimation than the other individual?

3. What basis or benchmark did the Pharisee use for comparing himself to others?

4. What does Christ mean when He says, “those who exalt themselves will be humbled, and those who humble themselves will be exalted?"

5. Do you have any capabilities and talents you can honestly say were developed by you from scratch without help or assistance from anyone?

(see 1 Corinthians 4:7 for help)

Application

Consider an aspect of life where you're prone to compare yourself favorably to others or consider yourself superior in any way.

How does that detract from your ability to relate to and connect with others in a positive way?
