Character Series

Honesty

Most of us won't say that we are dishonest..

but if we're honest about our honesty, we've all told lies at some point in the past.

What is honesty?

The definition of honesty is acting in a trustworthy way so that you will respect yourself and be respected by others.

Why be honest?

Socially, people trust you more and will believe on what you are saying. They won't have to second guess and question your intentions.

Why be honest?

Mentally, you can live confidently in who you are and what you say. You won't have to worry about getting your lies in order because the truth will always stay the same.

Why be honest?

Spiritually, it's the right thing to do. Always living and speaking in truth will honor God and will be a good example for others.

How to build honesty

Being honest is about actions, words, and deeds that are always consistent with the truth. (Obviously easier said than done)

Reflection

  • What are some of the reasons that people lie, cheat or steal?
  • What are some of the drawbacks when others discover that we've not been honest with them?

Scripture

Acts 4:32-5:11

1/5

Ananias and Sapphira had lethal consequences for their dishonesty, but is there ever a time when there are no consequences for being dishonest?

2/5

When we tell a lie, where does it come from (Acts 5:3)? Does it mean that it's a deliberate choice on our part when we lie, cheat, or steal (Acts 5:2)?

3/5

While we can fool some of the people some of the time when we're dishonest, do we ever fool God with our dishonesty? What does dishonesty do to our relationship with God?

4/5

Is it ever okay to tell little white lies? If yes or no, Why?

5/5

What are some benefits when we're being honest?

Application

What is one area of your life where you are prone to exaggerate or maybe even mislead others?

What possible motives did you have? Who possibly got hurt besides God when you are dishonest?

Whatever it may be, come to God with pure honesty in prayer and seek forgiveness and renewal.

1 John 1:9 assures us that God is ALWAYS “faithful to forgive us and to cleanse us from our sin.”
