2. Extra Giving

16 By this we know love, that He laid down his life for us, and we ought to lay down our lives for the brothers. 17 But if anyone has the world's goods and sees his brother in need, yet closes his heart against him, how does God's love abide in him? 18 Little children, let us not love in word or talk but in deed and in truth.

  • "Sees" - We need to proactively look for opportunites
  • "Closes his heart" - Don't give for the sake of giving. We give looking at our condition as we do so

Who are we giving to?

Give to everyone who begs from you, and from one who takes away your goods do not demand them back.

Luke 6:30

The giving mindset

  • Extra giving is not just looking around, but being intentional when doing so
  • To have something ready to give people

"A disciple does not ask 'How much can I keep?' but, 'How much more can I give?' Whenever we start to get comfortable with our level of giving, it's time to raise it again"

- Randy Alcorn

Don't focus on our standard of living,

but our standard of giving.

6 The point is this: whoever sows sparingly will also reap sparingly, and whoever sows bountifully will also reap bountifully. 7 Each one must give as he has decided in his heart, not reluctantly or under compulsion, for God loves a cheerful giver.

2 Corinthians 9:6-7

On the one hand, extra giving means being proactive and looking for opportunities.


But it also means looking at our heart condition. Not to feel good about ourselves or to appease our consciousness, but to give freely from the heart.

3. Extra Expressive

16 By this we know love, that He laid down his life for us, and we ought to lay down our lives for the brothers. 17 But if anyone has the world's goods and sees his brother in need, yet closes his heart against him, how does God's love abide in him? 18 Little children, let us not love in word or talk but in deed and in truth.

True love is extra expressive which means it must go beyond words.

"Christian love involves sacrifice and service. Christ did not simply talk about His love; He died to prove it."

- Warren Wiersbe

27 But I say to you who hear, Love your enemies, do good to those who hate you, 28 bless those who curse you, pray for those who abuse you.

Luke 6:27-28

16 By this we know love, that He laid down his life for us, and we ought to lay down our lives for the brothers. 17 But if anyone has the world's goods and sees his brother in need, yet closes his heart against him, how does God's love abide in him? 18 Little children, let us not love in word or talk but in deed and in truth.

Jesus didn't just die.. He died for us.

"In Truth"

  • Sacrifice without the actual need can be misguided
  • We can sacrificially love, but only just for the sake of it
  • We can be sincere, but sincerely wrong
  • But we're called to sacrificially love according to God's word
  • It's why the word "truth" is there

Ultimate Truth: Jesus

  • World's greatest need is salvation from our sins
  • Main focus is to bring glory to God
  • Jesus' act of sacrifice (because He loved us) did both

We should do the same when loving sacrifically and extra expressively by asking ourselves:

  • How can I love this person so that they might know the saving knowledge of Jesus?
  • How can I love this person to bring glory to God?

Example: Edric Mendoza's son

It wasn't the well constructed arguments and sound logic that convinced him


But it was because of the way his parents loved him

There's nothing comparable than showing love through action

Summary

  1. True Love is Extra Costly
    • Called to love willingly
    • Called to love sacrificially with meaning
  2. True Love is Extra Giving
    • Proactively seek to give extra
    • Give freely and from the heart
  3. True Love is Extra Expressive
    • Expressive love means action
    • True love is based on God's word and truth
